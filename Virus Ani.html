What is important to know in order to fight Nipah Virus and COVID-19?

Symptoms:



The most common symptoms include:- • Fever
• Chills
• Sore throat
• Muscle aches
• Severe fatigue or tiredness
• Runny or blocked nose, or sneezing
• Headache

Precautions:



• Keep a mask on your face which should cover your nose, mouth and chin.
• Keep washing your hands at regular intervals
• Maintain social distance
• Avoid having physical contact
• Store your mask in a dry and clean place

Cases and Deaths:



• Approximately 4,49,98,035 (four crore forty-nine lakh ninety-eight thousand and thirty-five) confirmed cases of infection in India.
• Approximately 5,31,930 (five lakh thirty-one thousand nine hundred and thirty) confirmed deaths in India.

Vaccination Status:



• India has administered over 2.2 billion doses overall, including first, second and precautionary (booster) doses.
• 95% of the eligible population (12+) has received at least one shot.
• 88% of the eligible population (12+) is fully vaccinated.

COVID-19

Symptoms:


• Fever
• Headache
• Muscle pain
• Sore throat
• Vomitting

Precautions:


• Practice handwashing regularly with soap and water.
• Avoid contact with sick bats or pigs.
• Avoid eating or drinking products that could be contaminated by bats, such as raw date palm sap, raw fruit, or fruit that is found on the ground • Avoid contact with the blood or body fluids of any person known to be infected with Nipah Virus.

Cases and Deaths:



• 6 (six) confirmed cases of infection in India.
• Approximately 2 (two) confirmed deaths in India.

History:


• The first case of Nipah virus happened when people who were in contact with infected pigs began getting very sick.
• Researchers then determined bats were the original source, having passed the virus to pigs.
• If an infected bat or pig spreads its bodily fluid to another animal, they infect that animal.
